### 5.4 Calibration

**Framing sentence.** Reliability diagrams for one (model, dataset) cell per model. ECE numbers across the full 12-cell grid live in Appendix C; §5.4 in the main paper carries the qualitative visual only.

**Bullets — write as 1 sentence each:**

- **Cells shown.** One reliability diagram per model. Default choice: HotpotQA-Llama and PopQA-Qwen, on the grounds that these are the two cells with the cleanest class balance and the highest absolute AUROC (so the calibration story is interpretable rather than dominated by class-imbalance artifacts). Lock the choice after `paper/data/calibration.csv` lands; recommend re-checking against ECE numbers before fixing.

- **Diagram format.** Standard reliability diagram: x-axis = predicted hallucination probability (binned, 10 bins), y-axis = empirical hallucination rate within bin, dashed diagonal as the perfect-calibration reference, bar widths proportional to bin counts. Overlay ours and one strong learned baseline (recommend ACT-ViT, since it is the §5.1 / §5.2 named comparator throughout §5) on the same diagram, distinguished by hatch pattern.

- **Score → probability mapping.** All scorers (KNN, cosine, Mahalanobis, ViT logits, MLP logits) are scalar outputs that are *not* native probabilities; we map to `[0, 1]` via the empirical CDF on the *training-set* scores per cell (already stated in [`04_experimental_setup.md`](04_experimental_setup.md) §4.5 ECE bullet). State this mapping once in the §5.4 caption and forward to Appendix C for the alternative isotonic / Platt-scaling sensitivity analysis.

- **What the diagram is *not*.** It is not an argument that our method is well-calibrated *out of the box* — it almost certainly is not, and the §5.4 prose says so. The argument is operational: even with a coarse empirical-CDF mapping, the predicted score is monotone-faithful enough to threshold against (FPR@95 reported in Appendix C). The §8 discussion may pick this up if calibration is unexpectedly poor.

- **Pending data.** `paper/data/calibration.csv` does not yet exist; the calibration pipeline needs an aggregator script — see open question 3 below. **§5.4 prose is the second-most-blocked subsection after §5.1** on data availability.

**What §5.4 establishes for the rest of the paper.** Modest claim about score usefulness, not strong claim about calibration. Reader exits §5.4 knowing the predicted score is reasonable-but-not-perfect, ECE numbers live in Appendix C, and the §5 main paper is done.
